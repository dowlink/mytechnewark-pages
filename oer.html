What Are OER?

Open Educational Resources are freely available teaching and learning materials you can use, adapt, and share.

About OER
Free materials that work for your course

Open Educational Resources (OER) are teaching and learning materials that are freely available to use, adapt, and redistribute. Unlike commercial textbooks, OER can be customized to fit your course — and students access them immediately at no cost.

OER include full textbooks, individual chapters, articles, videos, simulations, and entire course packages. Many are peer-reviewed and regularly updated by academic communities.

Get Started in 4 Steps

Follow this quick process to find, review, and publish OER for your course.

1
Find

Search for OER using OpenStax, OER Commons, LibreTexts, or the Mason OER Metafinder. Start with your discipline and course level.

2
Review

Evaluate materials for quality, accuracy, and alignment with your learning objectives. Check the license to confirm you can use and adapt the content.

3
Add

Add materials to your course using Leganto or Canvas. Mix OER with library-licensed articles and e-books to build a complete reading list.

4
Publish

Make materials available to students before the semester begins. Publish your Leganto list to Canvas so everything is ready on the first day.
