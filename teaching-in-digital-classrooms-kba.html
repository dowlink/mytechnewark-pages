Teaching in Digital Classrooms

Digital Classrooms at Rutgers–Newark are designed to support flexible, technology-enhanced teaching in small and medium-sized learning spaces. These classrooms support in-person, synchronous, hybrid, and multimedia instruction with integrated presentation systems, classroom audio, and recording tools.

Getting Started in Your Digital Classroom

Follow these steps to start the digital classroom technology and prepare your presentation.

1
Log into the podium computer

Use your NetID and password to log into the podium PC. The podium computer should already be powered on.

2
Tap the touchscreen to start the AV system

Touch the control panel at the podium to wake the AV system and begin using the room technology.

3
Select your presentation source

Choose the source you want to display, such as the podium PC, your laptop, wireless presentation, or document camera.

4
Configure the display

Use the touch panel to select which screens show your content. Adjust the display layout if needed.

5
Adjust audio as needed

Use the touch control panel to set audio levels for the room speakers. A microphone is available in classrooms equipped with audio support.

6
Adjust room technology during class

Use the touch control panel to manage display output, audio levels, and source selection throughout class.

Classroom Technology Signs

These signs are posted in classrooms to identify available technology features at-a-glance. Look for them on the door, podium, or wall near the equipment they describe.

InstructorCam

Auto-tracking camera supports hybrid and recorded class sessions.

Adjustable Height

The podium height can be adjusted for accessibility and ergonomic teaching.

Wireless Projector

Students and instructors can present wirelessly using AirMedia.

Power Available

Standard power outlets are available at student seats and the podium.

Touch Screen

An interactive touch display is available for annotation and direct interaction with on-screen content.

Present Content Your Way

Choose the option that works best for your class.

Podium Computer

The classroom podium PC runs Windows with all standard Rutgers software pre-installed. Log in with your NetID to access slides, browsers, and applications.

Laptop (HDMI or USB-C)

Plug your laptop into the provided cable at the podium to present directly. HDMI adapters for USB-C and DisplayPort are available by contacting ATS. It is suggested you bring your own.

Wireless Presentation

Use AirMedia to present from any laptop or mobile device without cables. Follow the on-screen instructions displayed when no source is active.

Touch Displays

Some digital classrooms include interactive touch displays that allow annotation, drawing, and direct interaction with on-screen content.

InstructorCam, Classroom Capture, Hybrid Teaching & FITS

InstructorCam & Hybrid Teaching

LIFU CAM, equipped with InstructorCam, can support hybrid and in-person class delivery. The auto-tracking camera follows the instructor throughout the front of the room.

  • Live-stream to remote students
  • Record class sessions
  • Auto-follow camera tracking
  • Hybrid-ready AV setups
  • Works with Zoom & Kaltura

Classroom Capture

Capture video and audio of your digital classroom session with Kaltura. Recordings are stored in your Kaltura folder and sync to Canvas automatically.

  • Automatic recording available
  • Students access via Canvas
  • Add captions and chapters
  • Create lecture highlights

Teaching Online & Hybrid

Make the most of your digital classroom for courses with remote participants. Zoom-enabled rooms let you see remote students on screen while teaching in-person.

  • Set up Zoom for hybrid delivery
  • See students on classroom display
  • Use captions when recording
  • Share class materials in Canvas

Faculty Innovation & Teaching Studio (FITS)

Explore, practice, and create with media tools and flexible spaces designed to support in-classroom teaching.

  • Try out digital classroom AV
  • Record a practice session
  • Test hybrid teaching workflows
  • Get one-on-one tool training
  • Work with an instructional designer
