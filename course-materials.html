Choose Your Course Materials Approach

Use commercial materials, library-supported materials, OER, or a purposeful blend of all three.

Approach 1
Adopt Publisher or Bookstore Materials
Use textbooks, digital learning platforms, publisher course packs, First Day materials, or other commercial resources available through the Rutgers bookstore or direct publisher access.
  • Submit required textbooks and publisher materials by the adoption deadline
  • Use publisher digital platforms such as WileyPLUS, Cengage, Pearson, or McGraw Hill
  • Confirm any Canvas courseware integration before students need access
  • Use First Day / Inclusive Access when discounted digital delivery is available
  • Tell students exactly where and how to access required materials
Wiley Cengage Pearson McGraw Hill
Review Adoption Options →
Approach 2
Use Library, OER, or No-Cost Materials
Use open textbooks, library e-reserves, licensed articles, databases, videos, and other materials students can access at no additional cost through Leganto and Canvas.
  • Find OER using OpenStax, OER Commons, LibreTexts, and discipline-specific repositories
  • Build a Leganto reading list with library-licensed books, articles, and media
  • Publish reading lists directly in Canvas
  • Use library e-reserves for chapters, articles, and other course readings
  • Verify licensing and access before sharing materials with students

Course Accessibility in Canvas

Ensure all course materials are accessible to every student. Use Blackboard Ally and Canvas accessibility tools to identify and fix accessibility issues before your course begins.

  • Check accessibility scores with Blackboard Ally
  • Fix inaccessible PDFs and scanned documents
  • Add alt text to images in Canvas

Course Materials Throughout the Semester

Use this lighter checklist to keep materials available, organized, and accessible from planning through course wrap-up.

Step 1
Before Semester
Build your foundation
  • Select textbooks, OER, library materials, or publisher tools
  • Submit textbook adoptions by the deadline
  • Build Canvas modules or a Leganto reading list
  • Check accessibility before publishing
Step 2
First Weeks of Class
Help students access materials
  • Confirm students can access required materials
  • Publish Canvas modules and reading lists
  • Announce where students should begin
  • Resolve access issues early
Step 3
During the Semester
Adapt and respond
  • Add supplemental resources as needed
  • Update readings based on class progress
  • Communicate material changes clearly in Canvas
  • Replace inaccessible materials with better alternatives
Step 4
End of Semester
Review and refine
  • Note what worked and what to improve
  • Review student access and affordability issues
  • Update reading lists and Canvas materials for next term
  • Explore OER alternatives for paid materials

Ways to Build Your Course Materials

Mix and match approaches to support your students and your course goals.

📖
Use Only Free Open Materials
Replace all paid materials with free, openly licensed resources from OpenStax, LibreTexts, OER Commons, or other discipline-specific collections.
📚
Combine OER and Library Resources
Combine open textbooks with library e-reserves, licensed articles, streaming media, and e-books for a no-cost course.
🔀
Mix Paid and Free Materials
Use a low-cost publisher text alongside free OER or library resources to balance cost, coverage, and course goals.
🏛️
Use Library Reserves for Readings
Build your reading list through Leganto using library-licensed books, articles, chapters, and media.
💻
Use Publisher Courseware
Use a publisher platform for integrated readings, homework, quizzes, and gradebook sync in Canvas.
✏️
Create Your Own Course Materials
Develop original notes, videos, practice activities, and Canvas pages to reduce or replace commercial materials.
