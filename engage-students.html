Teaching Strategies for Engagement
Set Clear Expectations
Define participation norms, response times, and communication channels in your syllabus. Students engage more consistently when expectations are explicit from day one.
Encourage Two-Way Communication
Use office hours, Canvas Inbox, and discussion boards to create dialogue, not monologue. Students are more likely to participate when they feel heard and valued.
Message Key Students
Use Canvas New Analytics to identify at-risk students and send targeted outreach early in the semester. A brief, personal message can significantly improve retention and engagement.
Leverage Technology
Use Canvas Announcements, Calendar reminders, and EchoPoll to stay connected at scale. The most effective instructors use technology to reinforce relationships, not replace them.
Active Learning Approaches
Design activities that keep students doing, thinking, and applying throughout class — think-pair-share, EchoPoll knowledge checks, case studies, and collaborative problem-solving work well in any format.
