Use PowerPoint Live in Microsoft Teams

How to present slides directly inside a Teams meeting using PowerPoint Live — including presenter tools, audience navigation, accessibility features, embedded media, hybrid tips, and troubleshooting.

Category:  Teach in the Classroom
Audience:  Faculty & Staff
Last reviewed:  May 2026
Reviewed by:  TLS, Rutgers–Newark
PowerPoint Live Microsoft Teams Presentations Hybrid Teaching Accessibility Microsoft 365

What Is PowerPoint Live?

PowerPoint Live is a presentation mode built into Microsoft Teams. Instead of screen sharing a PowerPoint window — which shows participants your desktop and application chrome — PowerPoint Live uploads your slides directly into the meeting as a native Teams experience. Participants see a clean, dedicated slide view; you see your presenter notes, upcoming slides, and meeting controls privately.

Traditional Screen Share
  • Participants see your desktop and taskbar
  • Notifications can interrupt the presentation
  • Presenter notes visible if not careful
  • Participants can't navigate independently
  • No built-in captions or translation
  • Performance degrades on slow connections
PowerPoint Live
  • Clean slide view — no desktop clutter
  • Notifications never appear to participants
  • Presenter notes stay private
  • Participants can navigate slides independently
  • Live captions and translation built in
  • Streams efficiently at any connection speed

Before You Present

  • Finalize your presentation and save it to OneDrive — PowerPoint Live pulls files from OneDrive automatically
  • Test any embedded videos or audio clips in advance; media that plays fine locally may behave differently when uploaded to Teams
  • Confirm slide text is readable at smaller screen sizes — remote participants may be viewing on laptops or phones
  • Prepare your presenter notes if you plan to use them; they will be visible only to you during the presentation
  • Confirm you are signed into the correct Rutgers Microsoft 365 account in Teams

Starting PowerPoint Live

  1. Join the Teams meeting — Open the meeting normally from the Teams Calendar, Outlook, or a meeting link.
  2. Click Share in the meeting toolbar — The sharing panel opens at the bottom of your screen.
  3. Select your file under PowerPoint Live — Recent OneDrive presentations appear automatically. If your file isn't listed, click Browse to upload from your device or search OneDrive. Teams uploads the file directly into the meeting.
  4. Wait for the upload to complete — A progress indicator will appear for larger files. Do not close the panel or navigate away while uploading.
  5. Begin presenting — Once loaded, participants see your slides in a clean Teams view. Your presenter controls — notes, slide panel, and navigation — appear privately on your screen.
💡 Save to OneDrive First
PowerPoint Live works most reliably when your file is already in OneDrive before the meeting starts. Uploading a large file from your device mid-meeting can cause delays. Save or sync your finalized presentation to OneDrive in advance, then select it directly from the PowerPoint Live panel.

Presenter Tools

PowerPoint Live gives you a private presenter view that participants cannot see, plus in-meeting annotation tools to direct audience attention.

📝
Presenter Notes Your slide notes appear alongside the current slide in your private view. Participants see only the slide — never your notes.
🗂️
Slide Navigation Panel Thumbnail previews of all slides appear in your presenter view. Jump to any slide at any time without participants seeing you navigate.
🔴
Laser Pointer A virtual laser pointer lets you highlight specific areas of a slide in real time for all participants — without drawing permanently on the slide.
✏️
Annotation and Highlighting Draw, highlight, or annotate directly on slides during the presentation. Useful for emphasizing key points or working through problems live.
💬
Meeting Chat and Reactions The Teams chat and participant panel remain accessible while you present — monitor questions and reactions without leaving the presentation view.

Audience Experience

Participants receive a more flexible experience than traditional screen sharing. They can zoom in on slide content, use screen readers to access the slide text, view live captions or translations in their preferred language, and — depending on your settings — navigate to previous slides independently without disrupting where you are in the presentation.

♿
Accessibility Features Built In
PowerPoint Live automatically enables live captions and translated captions for participants — they can choose their preferred language without any action from you. Screen readers can access slide text directly. Participants on mobile devices get high-quality slide scaling that screen sharing cannot match. Use readable fonts, high-contrast colors, and descriptive alt text on images to get the most out of these features.
💡 Controlling Audience Navigation
By default, participants can navigate to previous slides independently. If you prefer everyone to follow your pace — for example during a timed assessment or step-by-step walkthrough — you can disable independent navigation in the PowerPoint Live presenter controls (Presentation Settings → Prevent participants from advancing slides).

Embedded Videos and Audio

PowerPoint Live supports embedded videos, audio clips, and animations. However, media that works perfectly in the PowerPoint desktop app may behave differently when uploaded to Teams — test everything before an important session.

  • Test all embedded media with a colleague before the actual meeting
  • Keep video files optimized — large, high-bitrate videos may stutter on upload or playback
  • Pause briefly before playing media so participants have a moment to prepare
  • If a video fails to play, have a backup plan — the direct URL or a separately shared file

Using Multiple Monitors

A two-monitor setup significantly improves the PowerPoint Live experience — you get a full presenter view on one screen while the Teams meeting controls stay visible on the other.

🖥️ Primary Monitor — Presentation
  • Slides and presenter notes
  • Slide navigation panel
  • Annotation and laser pointer tools
💻 Secondary Monitor — Meeting Controls
  • Teams meeting chat and questions
  • Participant list and reactions
  • Timer, agenda, or speaker cues

Hybrid Meetings

PowerPoint Live is especially well-suited for hybrid teaching and meetings because remote participants receive the same dedicated slide view as in-room participants — without depending on a camera pointed at a projected screen.

🏫
Hybrid Presenting with PowerPoint Live
Share your slides via PowerPoint Live even when projecting in the classroom — remote participants get a crisp, independently navigable slide view rather than a camera feed of your projection screen. Post the presentation file in the Teams meeting chat at the start of class so students have it for reference and review after the session. Repeat all in-room questions aloud before answering, and monitor the Teams chat throughout so remote students can participate on equal footing.

Troubleshooting

My presentation isn't appearing in the PowerPoint Live panel.
PowerPoint Live pulls files from OneDrive. If your file isn't showing, save it to your Rutgers OneDrive first, then return to the sharing panel — it should appear under recent files. If you still don't see it, click Browse to upload from your device directly. Large files may take a moment to process before they're selectable.
The file is uploading very slowly or getting stuck.
Large files with many high-resolution images or embedded videos take longer to upload. Try compressing images in PowerPoint (File → Compress Media / Compress Pictures) and re-uploading. If you're on Wi-Fi, move closer to the router or switch to a wired connection. Uploading the file to OneDrive before the meeting (rather than uploading from your device mid-meeting) is significantly faster.
Embedded video won't play or has no audio.
Confirm the video is fully embedded in the PowerPoint file — linked videos (not embedded) will not play after upload. Check that the video format is supported (MP4 is most reliable). If audio is missing, verify the Teams meeting audio is working and that the video's audio track is embedded, not just the video. As a fallback, share the video file separately in the meeting chat.
Participants say slides look blurry.
Blurry slides are usually a bandwidth issue on the participant's end, not the presenter's. PowerPoint Live scales slides to the participant's connection quality automatically. If this affects many participants, simplify slide design by reducing the number of large images and using text-based slides where possible. Avoid using high-resolution photos as full-slide backgrounds.
Participants are advancing slides on their own and getting ahead of me.
Open the PowerPoint Live presenter controls and go to Presentation Settings. Enable Prevent participants from advancing slides to lock everyone to your current slide. You can toggle this on and off during the presentation — for example, enabling independent navigation during a self-paced review segment and disabling it when presenting new content.

Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do
  • Save your presentation to OneDrive before the meeting
  • Use PowerPoint Live instead of screen sharing slides
  • Test all embedded video and audio before presenting
  • Use presenter notes — participants can't see them
  • Use readable fonts, high contrast, and clean layouts
  • Share the file in the meeting chat for participant reference
  • Monitor chat throughout the presentation
✕ Don't
  • Upload large files from your device mid-meeting — sync to OneDrive first
  • Rely on linked (non-embedded) videos — they won't play after upload
  • Use small text or low-contrast color schemes
  • Overload slides with text — remote viewers read at their own pace
  • Ignore remote participants during hybrid sessions
  • Forget to test embedded media before an important presentation
