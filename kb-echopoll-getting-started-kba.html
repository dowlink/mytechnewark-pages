Getting Started with EchoPoll

How To Engage Your Students RU-N & RBS Only EchoPoll · Canvas

EchoPoll is Rutgers University–Newark's enterprise classroom engagement platform, integrated directly with Canvas. This guide walks you through setup and your first poll — most faculty are up and running in under 10 minutes.

⚡ 5-Minute Quick Start

Four steps to run your first poll:

  1. 1 Enable EchoPoll in Canvas — Settings → Navigation → drag EchoPoll Registration into active items → Save
  2. 2 Create a Poll — Open EchoPoll → Create → Slide Poll Deck → select your question type
  3. 3 Launch During Class — Select Launch — students join instantly on any device
  4. 4 Review Results — Results appear in real time; download reports after class
⚑ RU-N & RBS Faculty Only EchoPoll is licensed exclusively for Rutgers University–Newark and Rutgers Business School faculty. This tool is not available at other Rutgers campuses. Contact ATS at atshelp@newark.rutgers.edu if you have questions about access.

Overview

EchoPoll is integrated directly with Canvas and allows instructors to create interactive learning experiences before, during, and after class. Faculty can use EchoPoll to:

  • Conduct live polls and word clouds
  • Check student understanding in real time
  • Track attendance and measure participation
  • Facilitate Q&A sessions
  • Deliver pre-class activities
  • Sync grades with the Canvas Gradebook

EchoPoll can be used in face-to-face, hybrid, and online courses.

Before You Begin

To use EchoPoll, confirm the following:

  • You are enrolled in a Canvas course as an instructor
  • Your course is published and available in Canvas
  • Students are enrolled through the Canvas course roster

EchoPoll automatically synchronizes course rosters from Canvas. No manual roster import is required.

Accessing EchoPoll

Launch from Canvas

  1. 1
    Open your Canvas course — log in at rutgers.instructure.com and navigate to your course.
  2. 2
    Select EchoPoll Registration — click EchoPoll Registration in the course navigation menu.
  3. 3
    Authorize access if prompted — click Allow to connect EchoPoll to your Canvas account. This is a one-time step.
  4. 4
    EchoPoll opens — your course and roster are automatically associated. No additional setup is required.
📋 EchoPoll Not Visible? If EchoPoll does not appear in your course navigation, follow the Enable EchoPoll steps below.

Enable EchoPoll in Your Canvas Course

If EchoPoll is not visible in your course navigation menu, enable it through Canvas Settings:

  1. 1
    Open your Canvas course
  2. 2
    Select Settings — click Settings in the course navigation menu.
  3. 3
    Select the Navigation tab — click the Navigation tab at the top of the Settings page.
  4. 4
    Locate EchoPoll Registration — find it in the hidden navigation items list.
  5. 5
    Drag EchoPoll into the active navigation area — move it above the divider line to make it visible.
  6. 6
    Select Save — scroll down and click Save. EchoPoll will now appear in your course navigation.

Create Your First Activity

EchoPoll offers several activity types. Choose the one that best fits your instructional goal:

☑️ Multiple Choice Poll

Quickly check understanding or gather feedback. The most common activity type for new users.

☁️ Word Cloud

Collect short responses and display common themes visually. Useful for brainstorming and icebreakers.

❓ Q&A

Allow students to submit and vote on questions during class or asynchronously.

✅ Attendance Activity

Verify attendance and participation with a time-limited response window.

🎟️ Exit Ticket

Gather feedback or check understanding before students leave class.

Launch Your First Poll

A good first activity is a simple knowledge or opinion check. Example:

📋 Example: Opening Poll

How familiar are you with today's topic?
· Very Familiar  · Somewhat Familiar  · Not Familiar at All

To launch this poll:

  1. 1
    Open EchoPoll — select EchoPoll Registration from your Canvas course navigation.
  2. 2
    Create a new activity — click Create → Slide Poll Deck.
  3. 3
    Enter your question and answer choices — type the question text and add your answer options.
  4. 4
    Save the activity — click Save to store the activity in your course.
  5. 5
    Select Launch — click Launch to open the poll for students.
  6. 6
    Ask students to join and respond — students join automatically if EchoPoll is in the course navigation. Results appear in real time.
📱 Student Access Students do not need to install an app. They respond in a web browser on any device. If your course navigation includes EchoPoll, students are enrolled automatically.

Review Results

After an activity concludes, you can:

  • View participation data and review individual responses
  • Download reports for your records
  • Identify misconceptions to address in the next class
  • Use results to guide discussion or adjust instruction

Sync Grades to Canvas

Certain EchoPoll activities can synchronize participation or activity scores with the Canvas Gradebook. Before enabling grade sync:

  • Verify that your Canvas course is connected to EchoPoll
  • Review your grading settings in EchoPoll
  • Confirm how participation will be calculated
📚 Grade Sync Guide For complete instructions on connecting EchoPoll scores to the Canvas Gradebook, see the EchoPoll Grade Sync Guide.

Common Uses for EchoPoll

🙋
Increase Participation

Allow every student to contribute, including those who may be reluctant to speak in class.

📊
Check Understanding

Use polling questions throughout a lesson to identify misconceptions and adjust instruction.

✅
Take Attendance

Use attendance activities to verify student participation efficiently.

💬
Encourage Discussion

Use poll results as a starting point for classroom conversations.

📝
Gather Feedback

Collect anonymous feedback about course content and learning experiences.

ATS Recommendation

💡 Start Simple For your first EchoPoll activity, use a single multiple-choice question during class — a prior knowledge check, opinion poll, attendance check, or exit ticket. Once you are comfortable with the platform, explore word clouds, Q&A activities, and participation tracking. Most faculty find that one or two polls per class session is the right starting point.
