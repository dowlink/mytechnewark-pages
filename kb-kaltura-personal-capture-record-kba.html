Recording with Kaltura Personal Capture

How To Teach in the Classroom Kaltura Personal Capture Lecture Recording Screen Recording Canvas

Kaltura Personal Capture lets you record your screen, webcam, and microphone in any combination, then publish recordings directly to Kaltura MediaSpace and share them with students through Canvas. This article covers the full recording workflow — from launch to Canvas.

Launch Kaltura Personal Capture

You can launch Kaltura Capture two ways:

  • From your computer — open Kaltura Capture from your Applications folder (Mac) or Start menu (Windows)
  • From Canvas — go to Account → My Media → + Add New → Kaltura Capture

After first launch, a small Kaltura Capture icon appears in your system tray (Windows) or menu bar (Mac) so you can start recordings quickly without going through Canvas each time.

Before You Record Open any applications or content you plan to capture before starting the recording. Close unnecessary apps and browser tabs to free up memory, silence notifications, and find a quiet space with good lighting if using your webcam.

Configure Recording Settings

The Kaltura Capture toolbar lets you choose which sources to record. Use the dropdown menus or icons to configure each source before hitting Record.

Source Options Notes
Screen Full Screen · Select Area · Off Default: Full Screen. Use Select Area to capture a specific window or portion of the screen.
Camera On · Off Default: On. The webcam feed does not display on your screen during recording — only in the final video. Cannot be toggled mid-recording in dual-source mode.
Microphone On · Off Default: On. If the mic icon appears grey with a slash, select it to enable. On Mac, Kaltura Capture cannot capture computer audio — only your microphone.
Mac Audio Limitation On Mac, Kaltura Personal Capture cannot record system audio — sound from videos, music, or other applications playing on your computer will not be captured. Only your microphone is recorded. If you need to include system audio, consider using a separate audio routing tool or record on a Windows computer.

Start and Stop a Recording

  1. 1
    Select the red Record button — a 3-second countdown begins, then recording starts.
  2. 2
    Monitor the duration timer — the elapsed time displays in the Recording Menu. This timer does not appear in the final video.
  3. 3
    Use the toolbar controls as needed — pause/resume, use annotation tools, or minimize the toolbar during recording.
  4. 4
    Select the Stop button (white/grey square) — you will see a confirmation: "Are you sure you want to stop this recording?"
  5. 5
    Select "Yes, Stop it" — you are taken to the Recording Management window to save and title your recording.
Cancel Permanently Deletes the Recording The X (Cancel) button during recording will permanently discard the session — it cannot be recovered. Only use Cancel if you want to completely throw away the recording. Use Pause if you need to step away temporarily.

Annotations During Recording

Kaltura Capture includes annotation tools you can use while recording to draw attention to key areas of your screen.

Tool Description
Pencil Draw or handwrite freely on screen. Good for circling or underlining content.
Arrow Place a directional arrow to point at specific content on screen.
Text Add a text box anywhere on screen — useful for labeling or adding commentary.
Cursor Highlight Visually highlights mouse clicks so viewers can follow what you are selecting.
Select Area Draw a box to highlight a specific region of the screen for emphasis.

Save and Upload Your Recording

After stopping, the Recording Management window appears. Enter your recording details and choose a save option.

  1. 1
    Enter a title — required. Use a clear, descriptive title including course name and date (e.g., HIST 201 — Week 4 Lecture — Oct 14). This is what students will see in Canvas.
  2. 2
    Add a description and tags — optional, but tags improve searchability in MediaSpace.
  3. 3
    Choose a save option — see the table below.
Option What It Does
Save & Upload Saves the recording and immediately uploads it to your Kaltura MediaSpace library. Recommended for most recordings.
Save Saves the recording to your local library only. Use this if you want to review or trim before uploading, or if you are on a poor network connection.
Delete Permanently discards the recording. Cannot be undone.
New Recording Starts a fresh recording immediately. Warning: the current unsaved recording will be lost.
Upload Tip You can close Kaltura Capture after selecting Save & Upload — the upload continues in the background. Do not sign out of Windows or shut down your computer until the upload completes. A progress bar shows upload status. Check MediaSpace the following day if you are unsure whether the upload finished.

Upload a Saved Recording Later

If you chose Save instead of Save & Upload, you can upload the recording at any time.

  1. 1
    Launch Kaltura Personal Capture.
  2. 2
    Select Manage — this opens your local recordings library.
  3. 3
    Select Upload next to the recording you want to upload.

Find Local Recording Files

If you need to locate your recording files directly on disk:

Windows
C:\Users\[username]\AppData\Local\Kaltura\Capture\
Mac
~/Library/Preferences/Kaltura/Capture/

Access and Share Recordings in Canvas

  1. 1
    Log in to Canvas and go to Account → My Media. Your uploaded recording appears at the top of the library.
  2. 2
    Embed in a Canvas course — navigate to the course, open a Page, Module, or Assignment, and use the Kaltura Media Gallery tool in the rich text editor to insert your recording.
  3. 3
    Check privacy settings — recordings default to Private (only you can view). Publishing or embedding in Canvas makes them accessible to enrolled students.
Privacy Default All Kaltura recordings default to Private when uploaded — only you can see them. Embedding a recording in Canvas makes it accessible only to students enrolled in that specific course. To share more broadly, change the privacy setting in MediaSpace to Unlisted or Published.

Best Practices

Before Recording

🎬
Do a test recording first

Run a short test a few days before your first session to confirm audio and video quality, then a quick 30-second test at the start of each new session.

💻
Close unnecessary apps

Free up memory and avoid notification pop-ups. Silence your phone and desktop notification sounds before hitting Record.

🌐
Check your connection

Ensure good internet connectivity before uploading. A wired connection is more reliable than Wi-Fi for large files.

During Recording

📋
Have all content open and ready

Open slides, browser windows, and documents before selecting Record. Don't navigate away to find files mid-session.

✏️
Use annotation tools

Drawing attention to key points on screen significantly improves viewer engagement and comprehension.

🎙️
Speak clearly and at a measured pace

Microphone recordings benefit from slightly slower delivery than live lectures. Students can always replay, but clarity matters.

After Recording

▶️
Preview before sharing

Watch the recording in MediaSpace before sharing with students to catch any audio or video issues.

🏷️
Add descriptive titles and tags

Makes recordings findable in MediaSpace searches, especially as your library grows over the semester.

📣
Post a Canvas announcement

Add a brief announcement when new recordings are posted so students know to look for them.

Troubleshooting

Problem What to Do
Error starting or stopping a recording Check that camera and microphone permissions are enabled in your system settings. Restart the computer and relaunch Kaltura Capture. If the error persists, reinstall the application.
Camera does not appear or shows wrong camera Select the rectangle in the top-left of the toolbar to open camera source options. Select the correct camera. Confirm the camera is enabled in system Privacy settings.
Microphone icon is grey or shows a slash Select the microphone icon to enable it (it should turn blue/active). If it remains greyed out, check Privacy settings to confirm Kaltura Capture has microphone access.
Recording not appearing in MediaSpace after upload Check the upload progress bar. Uploads can take time for longer recordings — a 75-minute lecture may take 20–30 minutes. If you shut down during upload, reopen Kaltura Capture, go to Manage, and re-upload the local file.
No system audio in the recording (Mac) This is a known limitation on Mac — Kaltura Personal Capture cannot capture computer audio, only microphone input. Plan your content accordingly, or use a Windows computer if system audio is essential.
Students cannot find the recording in Canvas Confirm the Canvas page or module item is published. Check that the Kaltura embed is not restricted to a future availability date. Students must be enrolled in the course to view embedded recordings.
