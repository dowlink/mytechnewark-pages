EchoPoll Activity Ideas for Faculty

Best Practice Engage Your Students RU-N & RBS Only EchoPoll · Canvas

EchoPoll can be used throughout a class session to increase participation, gather feedback, check understanding, and promote active learning. This article provides ready-to-use activity examples that can be adapted to any discipline and integrated into existing lessons with minimal preparation.

⚑ RU-N & RBS Faculty Only EchoPoll is licensed exclusively for Rutgers University–Newark and Rutgers Business School faculty. This tool is not available at other Rutgers campuses. Contact ATS at atshelp@newark.rutgers.edu if you have questions about access.

Overview

Activities are organized by when they work best in a class session:

Before Class Beginning of Class During Class End of Class Mid-Semester Review Sessions

Before Class Activities

🌙 Before Class
Prior Knowledge Check
Determine what students already know before instruction begins.
Example Question"How familiar are you with today's topic?" · Very Familiar · Somewhat Familiar · Not Familiar
Best for: Gauging readiness and tailoring instruction
Reading Check
Verify that students completed assigned readings.
Example Question"Which concept from the reading was most surprising?"
Best for: Encouraging preparation and identifying discussion topics
Question Collection
Gather student questions before beginning a lesson.
Example Prompt"What question do you hope today's class will answer?"
Best for: Understanding student interests and concerns

Beginning of Class Activities

🌅 Beginning of Class
Attendance Check-In
Replace traditional roll call with a simple engagement activity.
Example Question"Which statement best describes your confidence with last week's material?"
Best for: Attendance and engagement
Icebreaker Poll
Learn about your students and build community.
Example Questions"What is your major?" · "What career interests you most?" · "What are you hoping to learn this semester?"
Best for: First week of class
Word Cloud Warm-Up
Ask students to respond with a single word or short phrase.
Example Prompt"What comes to mind when you hear the term 'leadership'?"
Best for: Activating prior knowledge

During Class Activities

🔍 During Class
Knowledge Check
Assess understanding immediately after instruction.
Example Question"Which explanation best describes the concept we just discussed?"
Best for: Identifying misconceptions
Prediction Poll
Ask students to predict an outcome before revealing the answer.
Example Question"What do you think will happen next?"
Best for: Building curiosity and engagement
Think-Pair-Share
A structured discussion technique that works especially well when responses are mixed.
  1. 1Ask a question
  2. 2Students submit an individual response
  3. 3Students discuss with a classmate
  4. 4Poll again
  5. 5Compare results before and after discussion
Best for: Discussion and conceptual learning
Opinion Poll
Present multiple viewpoints and ask students to choose a position.
Example Question"Which approach would you recommend?"
Best for: Debate and critical thinking
Scenario Analysis
Present a real-world situation and ask students to apply course concepts.
Example Question"What would be the most effective response in this situation?"
Best for: Application and problem-solving

End of Class Activities

🎟️ End of Class
Exit Ticket
Ask students to reflect on what they learned.
Example Question"What was your most important takeaway today?"
Best for: Reflection and retention
Muddiest Point
Identify concepts that remain confusing.
Example Question"What topic would you like reviewed next class?"
Best for: Planning future instruction
Confidence Check
Measure student confidence before leaving class.
Example Question"How confident are you in your understanding of today's material?" · Very Confident · Somewhat Confident · Not Yet Confident
Best for: Identifying learning gaps

Mid-Semester Feedback Activities

📅 Mid-Semester
Pace Check
Gather feedback about course pacing.
Example Question"How would you describe the pace of this course?" · Too Fast · About Right · Too Slow
Best for: Course adjustments and pacing decisions
Understanding Check
Example Question"Which topic would benefit from additional review?"
Best for: Identifying gaps and planning review sessions
Anonymous Feedback
Collect candid course feedback at mid-semester.
Example Prompts"What is one thing helping your learning in this course?" · "What is one thing that could improve your learning experience?"
Best for: Continuous course improvement

Review Session Activities

📝 Review Sessions
Exam Preparation Poll
Identify topics that need additional review before an exam.
Example Question"Which topic feels least familiar?"
Best for: Exam preparation and targeted review
Practice Questions
Use polling questions that mirror exam formats. Present a question in the same format students will see on the exam, collect responses, and review correct answers together.
Best for: Reinforcement and confidence building
Myth or Fact
Present statements and ask students whether they are true or false. Display a statement about a key course concept and ask: Is this statement true or false? After polling, reveal the answer and discuss why common misconceptions arise.
Best for: Correcting misconceptions before an exam

ATS Recommendation

💡 Start with Three Activities Per Session Many instructors find success using a check-in activity at the beginning of class, one knowledge check during instruction, and an exit ticket at the end of class. These three activities can significantly increase participation while providing valuable feedback about student learning. The most effective EchoPoll activities are brief, purposeful, and directly connected to course learning objectives.
