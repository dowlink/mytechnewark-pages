Using EchoPoll for Attendance Tracking and Participation

How To Engage Your Students RU-N & RBS Only EchoPoll · Canvas

EchoPoll allows instructors to verify attendance while simultaneously engaging students in learning activities. Rather than treating attendance as a separate administrative task, EchoPoll makes it a natural part of instruction — turning roll call into a moment of participation.

When to Use EchoPoll for Attendance

EchoPoll can be used to track attendance and participation in face-to-face, hybrid, and online courses. Attendance activities work best when they are integrated naturally into class rather than treated as a separate administrative task.

Use EchoPoll to:

  • ✓ Verify attendance at the start of class
  • ✓ Confirm participation throughout a lesson
  • ✓ Award participation points
  • ✓ Support active learning activities
  • ✓ Document attendance in large courses

Attendance vs. Participation

EchoPoll supports two related but distinct approaches. Many instructors find that participation-based attendance provides more meaningful information than a simple roll call.

Attendance

Confirms that a student was present. A check-in question at the beginning of class is sufficient. Simple and quick — one response per student per session.

Participation

Demonstrates that a student engaged with course activities. Students complete multiple activities throughout the session — polls, word clouds, exit tickets. Provides richer data than a single check-in.

💡
Recommended Approach
Participation-based approaches encourage more consistent engagement throughout a class session, rather than front-loading attendance at the start and disengaging after.

Best Practices

🎓
Make Attendance Part of Learning

Rather than asking "Are you here today?", ask students to think and respond. Examples: What is one thing you remember from last class? Which topic would you like reviewed? How confident are you about today's material? Students receive attendance credit while engaging with course content.

⚡
Use Low-Stakes Activities

Attendance activities should be quick and easy to complete — check-in polls, opinion questions, knowledge checks, or word clouds. The goal is to verify participation, not assess mastery. Keep it to one to two minutes.

📅
Be Consistent

Use a predictable attendance process throughout the semester. Students should know when attendance will be recorded, how participation is measured, whether points are awarded, and how missed activities are handled. Clear expectations reduce confusion and disputes.

📍
Consider Multiple Checkpoints

Use multiple activities during class to encourage continued engagement — an attendance poll at the start, a knowledge check mid-class, and an exit ticket at the end. This approach rewards participation throughout the lesson rather than only at the start.

📋
Communicate Your Policy

Include attendance and participation expectations in your syllabus. Students should understand how attendance is recorded, how participation is measured, how points are awarded, and what happens if technology issues occur.

Attendance Activity Ideas

These activity types integrate naturally into class as attendance or participation checkpoints.

Activity Ask Students Best For
✋ Course Check-In How are you feeling about this week's material? Beginning of class
❓ Prior Knowledge Poll How familiar are you with today's topic? Opening a lesson and gauging preparation
☁️ Word Cloud Check-In What word best describes your current understanding? Quick participation and reflection
📊 Mid-Class Knowledge Check A concept question midway through the lesson Verifying both attendance and continued engagement
🎟️ Exit Ticket What was your biggest takeaway from today's class? End-of-class participation and instructional planning

Using Participation Points

Many instructors choose to award participation points rather than attendance points. Participation activities can provide a more meaningful measure of student engagement than a binary present/absent record.

✅
Benefits of Participation Points

Encourages active engagement rather than passive presence. Rewards meaningful involvement with course activities. Supports active learning goals and reduces the emphasis on physical presence alone.

Attendance in Different Teaching Environments

🏫
Face-to-Face Courses

Use attendance activities at the beginning, middle, or end of class. Students respond on their own devices. Results are immediate and visible to the instructor in real time.

💻
Hybrid Courses

Use the same EchoPoll activity for both in-person and remote students. All students respond through the same link regardless of location, making hybrid attendance tracking straightforward.

🌐
Online Courses

Use participation activities during live Zoom sessions. Pair EchoPoll with Zoom chat or Breakout Rooms to create a fuller picture of student engagement throughout the session.

Common Challenges

Students join but do not participate

Consider using multiple activities throughout class rather than a single attendance poll at the start. Students who join late or disengage early are more likely to participate if there are multiple opportunities to respond.

Students arrive late

Establish and communicate a consistent policy — for example, students who respond within the first 10 minutes are marked present. Announce the policy in your syllabus and reinforce it at the start of the semester.

Technology issues

Provide guidance on how students should report technical difficulties and consider an alternative attendance method (email, sign-in sheet) for documented technology failures. EchoPoll access issues are rare but worth planning for.

Overemphasis on attendance

Attendance tracking should support learning objectives, not replace them. Activities are most effective when they are connected to course content — not when they exist solely to document presence.

💡
ATS Recommendation
The most effective EchoPoll attendance activities connect to course content, encourage participation, require minimal time, and provide useful feedback to instructors. Rather than asking students if they are present, ask them to think, reflect, and respond. Attendance becomes a learning moment rather than an administrative checkpoint.
