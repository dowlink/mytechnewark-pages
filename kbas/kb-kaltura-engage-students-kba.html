Using Kaltura to Engage Students in Canvas

How To Engage Your Students Rutgers Canvas · Kaltura · MediaSpace

Kaltura is Rutgers' enterprise video platform and is integrated with Rutgers Canvas. Because Kaltura is site licensed at Rutgers, it is available for use by Rutgers faculty and instructors without a separate departmental purchase or individual license.

At Rutgers–Newark, instructors can use Kaltura to create, upload, caption, and share videos directly inside a Canvas course. Kaltura can support active and flexible learning through short lectures, course introductions, demonstrations, narrated presentations, and student-created media assignments.

Common Uses for Kaltura

🎬
Welcome and Course Orientation Videos

A short welcome video helps students understand the structure of the course, where to begin, and how to get help. These videos work well on a Canvas Home Page or in a "Start Here" module.

  • Introduce yourself and the course
  • Explain how the Canvas course is organized
  • Highlight key expectations and deadlines
  • Help students feel connected before the first class meeting
📖
Short Lectures and Concept Reviews

Kaltura works best when videos are short, focused, and placed where students need them in Canvas. Instead of posting one long recording, consider creating several shorter videos — one concept, process, or example at a time.

  • A 5-minute explanation of a difficult concept
  • A narrated PowerPoint overview
  • A worked problem or demonstration
  • A review before an exam or assignment
  • A follow-up explanation after class
🎥
Student Video Assignments

Students can use Kaltura to record or upload video assignments in Canvas, giving them another way to demonstrate learning beyond written work.

  • Presentations and project pitches
  • Introductions and reflections
  • Group reports and demonstrations
  • Performances or creative work
💬
Media-Rich Discussions

Instructors and students can embed Kaltura videos in Canvas discussions to make activities more personal, visual, and interactive. Students might post a short video response, comment on a case study, or respond to a prompt using audio or video instead of text.

Kaltura Tools in Canvas

Kaltura appears in several places in Rutgers Canvas.

My Kaltura Media

Your personal video library. Recordings and uploaded videos are stored here and associated with your Rutgers NetID. Use it to upload videos, record new videos, manage captions, edit video details, and choose where videos should be shared.

Kaltura Course Gallery

The course-level video gallery. Videos in your personal library are not automatically visible to students — you must publish or embed them in the course. Use the Course Gallery when you want students to browse course videos from one place.

Kaltura in the Rich Content Editor

Embed Kaltura videos directly into Canvas pages, assignments, discussions, quizzes, and announcements. Embedding videos in a module or assignment usually creates a clearer student experience than sending students to a separate gallery.

Kaltura Personal Capture

Record your screen, webcam, microphone, or narrated presentation from your own computer. Use it for screen recordings, narrated slides, webcam introductions, software demonstrations, and short instructional videos.

Share a Video with Students

Use this workflow when you want to record, upload, and share a video in Canvas.

  1. 1
    Record or Upload Your Video
    Record a new video using Kaltura Personal Capture or upload an existing video file to My Kaltura Media. You can use Kaltura to share narrated PowerPoint presentations, screen recordings, webcam videos, demonstrations, short lecture clips, and existing video files you have permission to use.
  2. 2
    Review the Video Details
    Before sharing, review the title, description, and publishing settings. Use clear titles so students can understand what the video is and where it belongs in the course.
    Examples: "Week 3: Introduction to Research Methods" · "Module 4: Solving Linear Equations" · "Exam 1 Review: Key Concepts"
  3. 3
    Review and Edit Captions
    Kaltura can generate machine captions for videos. Captions should be reviewed and corrected — especially for required course content, technical terms, names, and discipline-specific vocabulary.
  4. 4
    Add the Video to Canvas
    You can share a video in Canvas in two common ways: embed the video in a Canvas page, assignment, discussion, quiz, or announcement; or publish the video to the Kaltura Course Gallery. For most instructional videos, embedding directly in the relevant Canvas module or assignment gives students the clearest path.
  5. 5
    Check Student Access
    After adding the video, use Student View or ask a colleague to verify that students can access the video from the intended location in Canvas.

Create a Student Video Assignment

Use this workflow when students need to submit a video as part of an assignment.

  1. 1
    Create the Assignment in Canvas
    Create a Canvas assignment with clear instructions, due date, points, and grading criteria. Include what students should record, expected length, whether students should appear on camera, whether captions or transcripts are required, how students should submit the video, and how the assignment will be graded.
  2. 2
    Tell Students to Use Kaltura
    Students can record or upload videos through Kaltura/My Media in Canvas and then submit the video to the Canvas assignment. Add a short instruction note such as:
    Example Note for Students

    Record or upload your video using Kaltura/My Media in Canvas. Submit your video through this Canvas assignment by the due date.

  3. 3
    Provide a Low-Stakes Practice Option
    For major video assignments, consider creating a short practice submission so students can test Kaltura before the graded assignment is due. This reduces last-minute technical issues and gives students time to ask for help.
  4. 4
    Review Submissions in Canvas
    Once students submit their videos, review and grade the assignment through Canvas. Use SpeedGrader if the videos are submitted through a Canvas assignment.

Accessibility and Captions

Accessible video helps all students, not only students with documented accommodations.

  • Review and correct machine-generated captions.
  • Use a clear microphone whenever possible.
  • Record in a quiet location.
  • Avoid relying only on visuals shown in the video.
  • Provide slides, handouts, or key materials in Canvas when appropriate.
  • Use descriptive titles so videos are easy to find.
📝 A Note on Machine Captions Machine captions are a helpful starting point, but they may not accurately capture names, technical terms, formulas, or discipline-specific language. Required instructional videos should be reviewed for caption accuracy before being shared with students.

Best Practices for Engaging Students with Video

✂️
Keep Videos Short and Focused

Short videos are easier for students to watch, review, and use while studying. Aim for one purpose per video.

📍
Place Videos Where Students Need Them

Embed videos directly in the Canvas module, page, assignment, or discussion where students are expected to use them.

💬
Tell Students What to Do While Watching

Add a prompt before or after the video so students know how to engage with it.

  • "As you watch, identify one concept that is still unclear."
  • "Pause after the example and try the next problem on your own."
  • "Post one question in the discussion after viewing."
  • "Complete the quiz after watching the video."
🔗
Pair Video with an Activity

Video works best when connected to an action. Consider pairing videos with a Canvas discussion, a short quiz, a knowledge check, a reflection prompt, a group activity, or a follow-up assignment.

🎯
Use Video Strategically

Not every course activity needs a video. Use Kaltura when video adds clarity, presence, demonstration, or flexibility that other formats cannot provide.
