Teaching in Auditoriums

Our auditoriums are designed for lectures, presentations, and events — and equipped with interactive technology to support in-person, hybrid, and recorded teaching.

Getting Started in Your Auditorium

Follow these steps to start the auditorium technology and prepare your presentation.

1
Log into the podium computer

Use your NetID and password to log into the podium PC. The podium computer should already be powered on.

2
Tap the touchscreen to start the AV system

Touch the control panel at the podium to wake the AV system and begin using the room technology.

3
Select your presentation source

Choose the source you want to display, such as the podium PC, your laptop, wireless presentation, or document camera.

4
Configure the display

Use the touch panel to select which screens show your content. Adjust the display layout if needed.

5
Turn on and use the microphone

Take the handheld or lapel microphone from the podium drawer or holder. Turn it on before class begins and test the audio in the room.

6
Adjust room technology during class

Use the touch control panel to manage display output, audio levels, source selection, and room lighting throughout class.

What You Can Do in Auditorium Spaces

🖥️
Present with Impact

Display content on multiple large screens and monitors throughout the auditorium.

🎙️
Be Heard Clearly

Use podium and ceiling microphones for clear room-wide audio amplification.

📡
Connect and Engage

Use InstructorCam and video conferencing tools to support hybrid instruction and remote participation.

👆
Touch Interactively

Use dual touch-screen interactive displays to annotate, interact, and control classroom displays.

📲
Share from Anywhere

Present from your laptop, phone, or computer wirelessly using AirMedia.

🎬
Record Your Class

Capture auditorium sessions including video using Kaltura Classroom Capture.

Auditorium Technology Signs

These signs are posted in auditoriums to identify available technology features at-a-glance. Look for them on the door, podium, or wall near the equipment they describe.

InstructorCam

Auto-tracking camera supports hybrid and recorded class sessions.

Adjustable Height

The podium height can be adjusted for accessibility and ergonomic teaching.

Wireless Projector

Present wirelessly from any device using AirMedia.

Power Available

Standard power outlets are available at student seats and the podium.

Touch Screen

An interactive touch display is available for annotation and direct interaction with on-screen content.

Present Content Your Way

Choose the source that works best for your class.

Podium Computer

The auditorium podium PC runs Windows with all standard Rutgers software. Log in with your NetID to access slides, browsers, and applications directly.

Laptop (HDMI)

Connect your laptop using the HDMI cable at the podium. USB-C and DisplayPort adapters are available. Your display will mirror to all auditorium screens.

Wireless Presentation

Share your screen wirelessly from any device using AirMedia. Follow the on-screen instructions displayed when no source is active.

Interactive Touch Displays

Use touch-enabled monitors to annotate slides, interact with content, and control classroom display from the front of the room.

InstructorCam, Course Capture & FITS

InstructorCam & Hybrid Teaching

Use InstructorCam for video conferencing, hybrid instruction, and remote participation in auditorium spaces. Auto-tracking camera follows the presenter.

  • Video conferencing support
  • Hybrid class delivery
  • Manual camera control
  • Clear audio for remote attendees
  • Works with Zoom, Teams, Webex

Course Capture

Record classroom sessions using Kaltura Classroom Capture. Captures audio, video, and presentation content automatically.

  • Capture audio, video, and presentation slides
  • Stores recordings in Canvas
  • Create review materials for students
  • Add captions and chapters
  • Review recordings in Canvas/Mediaspace

Faculty Innovation & Teaching Studio (FITS)

Practice and explore the same technology in a hands-on environment before your auditorium session.

  • Try InstructorCam and AV systems
  • Test hybrid teaching workflows
  • Practice auditorium presentation tools
  • Build confidence with new tools
