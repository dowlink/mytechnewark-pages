Classroom Wireless Success Guide

Steps for faculty and students to ensure a successful experience when using classroom wireless connectivity for assessments, polling, and other instructional activities. Rutgers–Newark classrooms are equipped with enterprise wireless infrastructure designed to support instruction — these steps help you use it effectively.

Category:  Teach in the Classroom
Audience:  F3 · ST1
Last reviewed:  June 2026
Reviewed by:  TLS, Rutgers–Newark
Wireless RUWireless Secure Classroom Assessments Polling Echo360 Student Devices

Because cellular coverage may vary by location within campus buildings, students should connect to RUWireless Secure rather than relying on a cellular data connection during classroom activities and assessments.

Before an In-Class Assessment or Activity

Faculty

  • Notify students in advance if a laptop or tablet is required for the course.
  • If an assessment relies on student devices and wireless connectivity, schedule a low-stakes test assignment or activity well in advance of the actual assessment date. This gives students sufficient time to verify device compatibility, confirm they can reliably connect to RUWireless Secure, and resolve any issues before the assessment.

Students

  • Configure your device to use RUWireless Secure before attending class.
  • Verify that your device is connected to RUWireless Secure before the assessment begins. Many smartphones, tablets, and laptops automatically switch to a cellular data connection when available. In some lower-level classrooms, interior spaces, or areas with limited cellular coverage, this can result in inconsistent connectivity or loss of access during an assessment. Students should confirm they are connected to RUWireless Secure and can successfully access required course resources before the day of the assessment.
  • Avoid using RUWireless Guest for classroom assessments or instructional activities — bandwidth and security restrictions may impact performance.
  • Ensure your operating system and web browser are updated before the day of the assessment.
  • Verify access to any required applications, websites, or course tools before class.
  • Arrive with your device fully charged. While many classrooms provide access to power outlets, availability may vary by room.
💡
Connect Before the Assessment Date
Students experiencing issues connecting to RUWireless Secure should contact the RUN-IT Service Center before the assessment date — not the morning of. Resolving connectivity issues in advance ensures you won't lose time during an assessment.

During an Assessment or Activity

  • Close unnecessary applications, browser tabs, and streaming services before beginning the assessment.
  • Do not download software, updates, videos, or other large files during the assessment period. Whenever possible, required downloads should be completed before class.
  • Follow any instructions provided by your instructor regarding device use and connectivity.

Contingency Planning

Faculty should consider a contingency plan for students who experience device-related issues during an assessment. Depending on the assessment type, this may include:

  • A paper version of the assessment
  • Access to an alternate device
  • A make-up assessment process
  • Other accommodations appropriate to the course
⚠️
Plan Ahead
Even with a properly configured device and campus wireless, unexpected issues can occur. Having a contingency plan communicated to students in advance reduces stress and ensures fairness when problems arise during assessments.

Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do
  • Connect to RUWireless Secure before class
  • Test connectivity ahead of assessment day
  • Update your OS and browser in advance
  • Arrive with a fully charged device
  • Close unnecessary apps and browser tabs during assessments
  • Faculty: schedule a practice activity before the real assessment
  • Contact the Service Center early if you have connectivity issues
✕ Don't
  • Use RUWireless Guest for assessments or polling
  • Wait until the day of the assessment to troubleshoot connectivity
  • Download large files or updates during an assessment
  • Leave streaming services or extra browser tabs open
  • Assume all classrooms have power outlets available
  • Skip the practice activity if your course relies on student devices

Additional Resources

For assistance with Rutgers wireless connectivity, refer to the guide on connecting to RUWireless Secure. For additional support, contact the RUN-IT Service Center.

Need Help?
Students experiencing connectivity issues before an assessment should contact the RUN-IT Service Center as early as possible. Waiting until the start of an exam may limit the available troubleshooting options.
